## Post Content

Nobody tells junior devs this — but your first job isn't about the salary.

It's about the reps.

Every bug you debug, every PR review you receive, every system you break and fix — that's compounding experience that no bootcamp or degree can replicate.

Here's what I wish I knew earlier:

**1. Ship something real, even if it's small.**
A live project says more than 10 certificates. A simple CRUD app, a bot, a personal tool — it shows you can finish things.

**2. Learn to read code before you write it.**
Most of your career will be spent understanding *existing* codebases, not building from scratch. Practice reading open-source projects.

**3. AI tools are a multiplier, not a replacement.**
Use GitHub Copilot, Claude, and ChatGPT — but understand what the output does before you paste it. The devs who thrive are the ones who *direct* AI, not the ones who blindly trust it.

**4. Document your wins publicly.**
LinkedIn, GitHub, a personal blog — it doesn't matter. Build a trail of your work. Future hiring managers and clients will find it.

You don't need to be a genius to break into tech. You need consistency and visibility.

What's one piece of advice you'd give your junior self? Drop it below 👇
